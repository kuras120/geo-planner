# Market Opportunity Assessment

Assessment date: 2026-07-19.

## Conclusion

The need exists, but “another parcel map” is not a durable advantage. The most
promising direction is a digital parcel dossier combining:

```text
CHECK → PLAN → MONITOR
```

- **Check:** collect official data and expose sources, dates, and limitations.
- **Plan:** create non-binding building, access, or subdivision scenarios.
- **Monitor:** detect new documents and planning changes affecting an area.

A layered viewer can be replaced by Geoportal, PLANIA, QGIS, or report
services. Project continuity, interpretable evidence, private scenarios, and
monitoring are harder to substitute.

## Confirmed Problem

Parcel analysis currently combines Geoportal, public-information portals,
planning documents, utility maps, and private materials. Public sources expose
data but rarely guide a decision; reports are often one-off, while professional
GIS is too complex for many owners, agents, and small investors.

Planning reform and standardized general-plan data improve availability while
reducing the defensibility of simple layer display.

## Promising Users

| Segment | Repeated need | Plausible model |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Small land investors | Portfolio comparison and monitoring | Subscription |
| Architects and advisers | Fast analysis and client communication | Professional tool |
| Land agents | Evidence-backed buyer communication | Subscription or report |
| Owners of larger sites | Access, utilities, subdivision, and plan changes | Project plus monitoring |
| One-time buyer | Check one parcel | Report or time-limited access |

## Product Hypotheses

| Hypothesis | Value | Main risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Create a project from parcel, address, or polygon | Removes manual source assembly | Uneven data quality and coverage |
| Explain parcel status | Interprets evidence instead of merely displaying it | False appearance of legal certainty |
| Monitor documents and zones | Creates repeat value | Change detection across fragmented sources |
| Model development scenarios | Supports early decisions | Must not resemble surveying or construction design |
| Compare parcels | Supports an actual purchase choice | Requires comparable evidence |
| Export and share privately | Improves collaboration | Privacy and interpretation liability |

Do not initially build a full GIS/CAD, automated legal analysis, land-register
system, public-administration product, or a national copy of all source data.

## Validation Before Expansion

Test what users pay for through recent-process interviews, several manually
delivered analyses, and separate offers for a report, interactive project, and
monitoring. Automate only after the leading use case is evident. Actual sources,
time/cost, fear of errors, existing payments, and repeated information checks
are stronger evidence than opinions about a hypothetical application.

## Risks

- Missing data does not prove absence; coverage and currency vary.
- Every conclusion needs a source, date, confidence, and legal/surveying caveat.
- Public portals may absorb basic presentation features.
- Commercial reports may add layers quickly; history and workflow are harder to copy.
- A one-off report alone rarely supports a long-term subscription.
