# Similar Applications Research

Research date: 2026-07-19.

## Conclusion

No single reviewed product combined Polish official data, private sketches,
account-free local storage, and self-contained HTML export. Strong substitutes
exist for individual parts of that workflow.

| Product | Primary strength | Gap relative to Geo Planner |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PLANIA | Parcels, drawing, measurements, GML/DXF, and export | Full source set and local workspace not confirmed |
| Geoportal.gov.pl | Current Polish official data and identification | Limited personal workflow and local projects |
| QGIS + QField | Full GIS, formats, automation, and offline use | High learning and configuration cost |
| Scribble Maps | Simple sketches and export | No built-in Polish official sources |
| Felt | Collaboration and modern map UI | Cloud-first and no Polish integrations |
| OnGeo / Działki360 | Automated risks and reports | No personal scenario editor |
| Geoportal Krajowy / Na Mapie | Fast parcel search and selection | Weaker project workflow |

The comparison measures fit for this workflow, not overall product quality.

## Implications

- A one-time parcel check may be served by Geoportal or a commercial report.
- PLANIA is the first practical comparator for sketching and GIS/CAD exchange.
- QGIS/QField remain the technical benchmark for a professional spatial system.
- A dedicated product is justified only if local data control, selected sources,
  simplicity, and continuity outweigh integration maintenance.
- Differentiation should be the evidence-to-scenario-to-history workflow, not
  merely the number of layers.

## Limitations

The review used public descriptions, not full tests of paid tiers, accounts,
offline policies, or every export. Refresh it before a product or purchasing
decision because features and prices can change.
